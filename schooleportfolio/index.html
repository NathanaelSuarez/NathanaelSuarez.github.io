Outcome 1: Generating Inquiry

I think that to me, Generating Inquiry is the process of turning a general curiosity into a focused and answerable question that can be answered through research. It also involves going from a vague idea to a solid question. This process happened when I was developing the research questions for my first project. My initial interest in the r/aliens subreddit was broad because I just thought that it was interesting how people came to believe conspiracies, and I was curious about government distrust. The "Turning RTs into RQs" activity was helpful, and it helped me get a concrete set of questions to work with. I considered what was actually observable and analyzable within Reddit comments and posts. This helped me get specific researchable questions. The final question I developed "How does ironic content on the r/aliens subreddit contribute to government distrust?" is a direct result of this focused inquiry process. This artifact demonstrates my growth because it shows the exact moment my broad interest was refined into a formal academic question.

Outcome 2: Information Literacy

I think I understand Information Literacy as the ability to evaluate a set of source's relevance, understand their arguments and figure out how they can support my project. It's about ethically and effectively putting sources into conversation with my ideas. My preliminary annotated bibliography, where I found and synthesized two academic sources, is the best artifact to show my progress here because I focused on what made each source useful to me. For example, with a nonacademic article, I identified the concept of irony poisoning becoming sincerity as a useful framework for my analysis, and I recognized that the methodology from the Godwin et al. study, which categorizes different memes into types like "Deception" and "Superiority," could provide to me a structured way for me to analyze the content on r/aliens. This process shows that I am actively evaluating different sources' methods and concepts to make myself a custom research plan, which is the definition of being information literate.
